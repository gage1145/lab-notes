## Purpose

The last round of Micro-QuIC failed due to the air pockets migrating into the channel. We will try to account for this migration by expelling as much air from the flow-cell as possible. We think that the compressible air was allowing the liquid to move which pulled the air from the side channels.

## Design
1. Completely fill the flow-cell with positive-spiked RT-QuIC master mix.
2. Tilt the flow-cell at an angle where the exit channel is at the top.
3. Pipette the liquid from the bottom. Do not plunge the pipet; slowly rotate the plunger instead.
	1. This seems to keep the side channels more intact.
4. 6500Hz, 42$\textdegree$C, 4hr
