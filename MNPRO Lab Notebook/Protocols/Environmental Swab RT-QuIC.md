## Equipment
- BMG plate reader
- PCR hood
- Tabletop centrifuge
- Cup horn sonicator
- Biosafety cabinet
- -20$\textdegree$C or -80$\textdegree$C freezer
- Vortex
- p1000
- p200
- p10 or p20
- p2

## Consumables
- Extraction
	- 0.6mM myristyl sulfobetaine (MSB)
	- Environmental swab
	- 1.5mL tube
	- 1000$\textmu$L filter tips
	- 200$\textmu$L filter tips
- RT-QuIC
	- 5X PBS
	- 2M NaCl
	- 100mM EDTA
	- 100mM thioflavin T (ThT)
	- HaPrP
	- Sample dilution buffer
		- 0.1% SDS
		- 1X PBS
	- 96-well plate
	- Plate tape

## Protocol
### Swab Extraction
1. Add 300$\textmu$L of MSB to the tube containing the swab.
2. Vortex briefly (~2s).
3. Sonicate in the cup horn
	- 3 cycles of 5sec on and 5sec off
	- Set to 50 for ~172🔽
4. Collect as much liquid as possible and add to a new tube.
5. Keep the tube containing the swab and store in the freezer.
6. Centrifuge the tube at 2,400xg for 3min at 4$\textdegree$C.
7. Carefully transfer the supernatant to a new tube.
8. Discard the pellet.
9. Centrifuge the supernatant at 21,100xg for 30min, 4$\textdegree$C.
10. Remove and discard the supernatant.
11. Store the pellet in the freezer or use immediately in RT-QuIC.
### RT-QuIC
#### Master Mix
12. In a 10mL tube add:
	- 2mL 5X PBS
	- 850$\textmu$L 2M NaCl
	- 100$\textmu$L EDTA
	- 100$\textmu$L ThT
13. Add the appropriate amount of HaPrP for the final concentration to be 0.1mg/mL.
14. Bring to 10mL with water.
#### Sample Dilutions
15. Resuspend swab pellets in 500$\textmu$L of sample dilution buffer.
	- This is considered a 10$^{-1}$ dilution.
16. Vortex vigorously.
#### Plate Setup
17. Pipet 98$\textmu$L of master mix into each well of the microplate.
18. Pipet 2$\textmu$L of the swab dilution to each well.
19. Seal the plate with plate tape before running.
